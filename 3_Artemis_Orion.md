# 🌕 1. Big Picture: What is Orion ESM?

## ✅ Core Idea

The **Orion spacecraft** is NASA’s vehicle for sending astronauts beyond low Earth orbit, mainly to the Moon under the **Artemis program**.

The **European Service Module (ESM)** is built by **Airbus for ESA** and provides the spacecraft with:

- 🚀 Propulsion
- ⚡ Electrical power
- 💧 Water and oxygen
- 🌡 Thermal control

It is the **first mission-critical element of a US crewed spacecraft built outside the USA**.

# 🔥 2. Main Functions of the ESM

## 🚀 Propulsion

The ESM provides propulsion for:

- Long-distance travel
- Orientation
- Navigation
- Orbital maneuvers

### Numbers to remember:
- **33 thrusters total**
- **8,000 liters of propellant**

---

## ⚡ Power Generation

The ESM uses solar arrays.

### Numbers:
- **15,000 solar cells**
- Four-wing solar panels
- Span: **19 meters**
- Width when deployed: **7 meters**

---

## 💧 Life Support Consumables

The ESM stores:
- 240 L water
- 90 kg oxygen
- 30 kg nitrogen

These support the astronauts during lunar missions.

---

## 🌡 Thermal Control

Protects astronauts from:
- Extreme cold
- Overheating

External temperatures can reach:
- **−270°C**

# 🌍 7. European Contribution

## ESA + Airbus Role

Europe contributes by building:
- Orion ESMs

In exchange, ESA receives:
- Astronaut seats on Artemis missions

This is called a:

# 🔄 “Barter System”

---

# 🤝 8. ISS Barter System

Before Artemis, ESA used a similar barter model for the ISS.

ESA contributed:
- Columbus module
- ATV cargo spacecraft

In return:
- Access to ISS facilities
- Astronaut time
- Scientific utilization rights

# 💼 10. The Space Economy

## Transition to Commercial LEO Economy

LEO = Low Earth Orbit

The presentation explains the transition from:
- Government-operated stations (ISS)
➡
- Commercial stations and infrastructure

### Key ideas:
- Agencies become “anchor customers”
- Commercial companies operate stations
- Industry uses space for manufacturing & R&D
