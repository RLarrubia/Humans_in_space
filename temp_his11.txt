           ‒ psychological
           ‒ medical

Space Training Team                                                 Slide 25
 Generic Robotics Training (GRT)
 At EAC a team of 3 instructors teach astronauts the basics
 of robotics in order to manipulate the ISS Robotic Arm


  Training focuses on:
      ‒ Arm presentation and roles in ISS ops
      ‒ Understanding the concept of parallax
      ‒ Clearances monitoring and cameras usage
      ‒ Understanding various robotics frames
      ‒ User input mastery
      ‒ Target use & alignment
      ‒ Manual mode vs. single joints or automatic modes
      ‒ ISS familiarization in relation with robotics
      ‒ Task efficiency and situational awareness
      ‒ Verbalisation and operational setup

Space Training Team                            Slide 29
 Extra Vehicular Activity (EVA) Proficiency Training
 •    The Neutral Buoyancy Facility contributes to the maintenance of ESA astronauts EVA skills
      in Europe – no need to travel to Houston
 •    Astronauts train standard EVA operations in simulated weightlessness while operating real
      EVA tools and applying EVA rules for safety and efficiency
 •    In Pre-Assignment Training ESA astronauts systematically receive refresher training before
      resuming an EVA suited run at NASA




Space Training Team                                                                         Slide 30
 Parabolic Flights
   An ESA developed training programme to expose ESA astronauts to the challenges of living
   and working in weightlessness before their first flight assignment, thereby increasing their
   efficiency during the first days on board ISS

 • Typical activities include:
    ‒    Moving efficiently in weightlessness
    ‒    Handling of equipment, crew bags and tools
    ‒    Coping with disorientation illusions
    ‒    Body stabilisation when operating hardware
    ‒    Cardiovascular resuscitation in weightlessness
    ‒    EVA tether protocols

 • During ISS mission for:
      −     regular emergency drills (fire, depressurisation, toxic spill),
            combined with Flight Control Teams
      −     proficiency maintenance of critical operational skills, e.g. rendezvous & docking

Space Training Team                                     Slide 49
 Human Behaviour and Performance (HBP)

 • Refers to how behaviour affects human performance
 • Promotes the use of behaviours which enable optimal use of all available
   resources (equipment, procedures, people) to enhance team safety and
   operational effectiveness
 • Based on operational and social psychology, uses the methods and
   techniques of Crew Resource Management
 • ESA HBP training is given to Astronauts (during Basic or Pre-Assignment
   training), Eurocoms, Flight Directors and Flight Controllers, so that all these
   different groups have the same understanding and are able to work together in
   minimising the potential for ‘human error’

 Challenges of ISS Training

The planning, coordination & implementation of ISS Training poses some challenges:

 •    Complexity of the International ISS consortium
       ‒ Crew exchanges
       ‒ Multitude of on-board tasks
             fair distribution across 6 crew members

 •    Number of International Partners
       ‒ Two languages on-board
       ‒ 5 distinct, globally distributed training locations
       ‒ Cultural differences

 •    Time Pressure
       ‒ Restriction of Assigned Crew Training to 24 months
       ‒ Dynamic development of tasks requiring constant training updates
